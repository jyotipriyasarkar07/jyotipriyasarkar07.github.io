About Me

Driven by curiosity,
guided by molecular biology

I am a researcher in the Functional Amyloid Biology Lab at the Department of Biosciences and Bioengineering, IIT Jodhpur. My work sits at the interface of protein biochemistry, cell biology, and neuroscience.

My primary focus is on amyloid and functional amyloid biology — studying how proteins adopt fibrillar architectures and how these structural transitions are linked to disease. I am particularly fascinated by the phenomenon of Liquid–Liquid Phase Separation (LLPS), whereby proteins and nucleic acids condense into membraneless organelles.

When this process goes awry, it can seed toxic protein aggregates implicated in neurodegenerative disorders such as ALS, Parkinson's disease, and Alzheimer's disease. I believe decoding the molecular grammar of phase separation holds the key to understanding — and potentially reversing — some of the most devastating diseases of our time.

Research Interests

What I am curious about

💧

Phase Separation

How intrinsically disordered proteins undergo LLPS to form biomolecular condensates, and how aberrant phase transitions drive pathological aggregation.

🧬

Amyloid Biology

Characterising fibrillar protein architectures — both functional amyloids that serve cellular roles and pathological ones linked to neurodegeneration.

🧠

Neurodegenerative Disorders

Linking protein aggregation and condensate dysregulation to the molecular progression of ALS, Parkinson's disease, and Alzheimer's disease.

🧩

Gene Regulation

How transcription factor condensates and RNA-binding proteins participate in gene regulatory networks, and how mutations disrupt these processes.
